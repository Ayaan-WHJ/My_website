Asanas

Some Asanas are :

1 : Tadasana -

1)Stand straight on the ground, and take a small gap between your feet.

2)With deeply breathing (inhale), raise your both arms.

3)Keep your arms upward by interlocking your fingers.

4)Now come on the toes by raising your heels simultaneously



2 : Vrikshasana -
1)Stand tall and erect with your arms by your side and shoulders relaxed.

2)Bend your right leg and place your right foot high up on your left thigh.

3)The sole of your right foot should be placed flat and firmly on the inner side of the thigh. And your left leg should be straight

4)Once you have found your balance, take a deep breath and gracefully raise your arms over your head. Bring your palms together in ‘namaste’ mudra i.e. hands folded.

5)Look straight ahead at a distant object, with a steady gaze. Make sure your spine in straight, and keep taking long deep breaths. Relax the body more and more each time you exhale, and try to hold the pose for as long as possible.

6) Then slowly exhale, bring down your hands from the side of your body and gently release the right leg.

7)Stand tall and straight as you did before you started, and repeat the pose with your left leg while your right leg rests on the floor.





3 : Bhujangasana -

Steps(bhujangasan)

1)Place your palms flat on the ground directly under your shoulders. Bend your elbows straight back and hug them into your sides.

2)Pause for a moment looking straight down at your mat with your neck in a neutral position.

3)Inhale to lift your chest off the floor. Roll your shoulders back and keep your low ribs on the floor. Make sure your elbows continue hugging your sides.

4)Keep your neck neutral. Don’t crank it up. Your gaze should stay on the floor.



4 : Parvatasana(Seated) -

1)Sit straight up and assume Padmasana.

2)Intertwine the fingers of both hands to form a finger lock.

3)Stretch the arms so that they are straight.

4)The posterior and knees should remain on the ground.

5)Turn up your palms over the head.

6)Exhale.

7)Inhale and now and stretch your arms further up feeling the tug on the shoulder blades.

8)Stretch your hands upwards and remain in this posture for about ten seconds.

9)Exhale and return to Padmasana.

10)You can also perform the pose with the palms joined together and fingers stretched up.





5 : Dhanurasana -

1)Lie down on the ground on your belly.

2)Bend the knees backward.

3)Hold the feet with hands below the ankles. Take a deep breath and raise the chest as high as possible.

4)Now stretch the feet, so that body takes the shape of a bow. Stay in this position as long as you can. While breathing out, relax the body and come in the initial position. Repeat this Aasan for 3-4 times.
